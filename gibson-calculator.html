5. Bill's three rules (reference)

  1. Rule 1 — pmol cap. Total DNA in the reaction ≤ 0.01 pmol/µL of reaction volume. That's ≤ 0.05 pmol in a 5 µL rxn and ≤ 0.2 pmol in a 20 µL rxn. (Bill's Apr 14, 2026 correction — he first wrote 0.1 and corrected himself.)
  2. Rule 2 — 40 % carry-over cap. Sum of fragment volumes ≤ 40 % of total reaction (≤ 2 µL in 5 µL; ≤ 8 µL in 20 µL). Dilution with water doesn't count — only the undiluted-equivalent volume is charged against the cap. So diluting an over-concentrated fragment literally buys you room.
  3. Rule 3 — Ice → 50 °C, no warming. Set up on ice, transfer directly to the 50 °C block. Don't let the tube reach room temperature. Higher starting T disfavors excessive 5' exonuclease chew-back (this is what makes HiFi accurate). Bill's most common past failure mode.

6. Protocol (after pipetting)

  1. Pipette the recipe above on ice. Water first, then fragments, then 2× HiFi master mix (thawed, pulse-vortexed, spun).
  2. Transfer directly to 50 °C. Hold 15 min for 2-3 fragments or 60 min for 4-6 fragments.
  3. Put on ice (or freeze at -20 °C) until transformation.
  4. Transform 1-2 µL of chilled reaction into 25-50 µL NEB 5-alpha (or 10-beta if > 10 kb). 30 min on ice → 42 °C × 30 s → 2-5 min ice → add 950 µL SOC → 37 °C, 250 rpm, 60 min → plate on amp. Overnight at 37 °C.

Math: pmol = ng × 1000 / (bp × 650), assuming 650 Da/bp for dsDNA. Equivalently fmol/µL = (ng/µL × 1000) / (bp × 0.650).
